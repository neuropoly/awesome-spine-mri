# 📂 Spine MRI Data Repositories

This list contains the primary databases and specific datasets used for research in spine imaging, vertebrae segmentation, and degenerative disease classification.

### 🏛️ Global Imaging Databases
* **[OpenNeuro](https://openneuro.org/)** – A free and open platform for sharing BIDS-compliant MRI data.
* **[The Cancer Imaging Archive (TCIA)](https://www.cancerimagingarchive.net/)** – High-quality DICOM images, specifically useful for spinal oncology and metastasis.
* **[Kaggle Datasets](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets)** – Hosts large-scale clinical competition data, including the massive RSNA 2024 collection.
* **[Grand Challenge](https://grand-challenge.org/)** – The primary hub for medical imaging benchmarks and standardized segmentation tasks.
* **[Mendeley Data](https://data.mendeley.com/)** – An open repository for raw data associated with specific academic publications.
* **[Zenodo](https://zenodo.org/)** – A general-purpose repository used by researchers to host curated, paper-specific spine datasets.

### 🔬 Specialized Spine & Spinal Cord Resources
* **[Spinal Cord Toolbox (SCT) Datasets](https://spinalcordtoolbox.com/user_section/tutorials/datasets.html)** – Specialized data for spinal cord segmentation and template-based analysis.
* **[Stanford AIMI](https://aimi.stanford.edu/shared-datasets)** – Curated musculoskeletal (MSK) datasets from Stanford University.
* **[PhysioNet](https://physionet.org/)** – Provides complex clinical imaging data from hospital settings.
* **[Hugging Face Datasets](https://huggingface.co/datasets)** – A growing repository for AI-ready imaging and pre-processed spine MRI subsets.
